Term Test 2 Supplementary Practice

Q1: Definitions

Recalling Definitions

Write down the defintions for the following:

  1. Intermediate Value Theorem

  2. Extreme Value Theorem

  3. Critical Point

  4. Inflection Point

  5. Continuity at a point

  6. Limit definition of a derivative

  1. If a function \(f(x)\) is continuous on \([a, b]\) and \(N\) is between \(f(a)\) and \(f(b)\), then there exists a number \(c\) such that \(f(c) = N\).

  1. If a function \(f(x)\) is continuous on \([a, b]\), then \(f(x)\) must attain both an absolute maximum and an absolute minimum on \([a, b]\).

  1. Point \((c, f(c))\) is a critical point if \(c\) is in the domain of \(f\) and \(f'(c) = 0\) or \(f'(c)\) is undefined.

  1. Point \((c, f(c))\) is an inflection point if \(f(x)\) is continuous at \(c\) and the concavity changes at \(c\).

  1. A function \(f(x)\) is continuous at \(x = a\) if \(\;lim_{x \to a} f(x) = f(a)\).

  1. \(f'(x) = lim_{h \to 0} \frac{f(x + h) - f(x)}{h}\)

Rewriting in the Form of “if … then …”

Some of the statements above can be written in the form of a conditional statement; that is, in the form of “if {condition} then {conclusion}”, e.g. if \(x > 0\) then \(x > -1\).

Q2: Continuity & IVT

Showing that Roots exist

  1. Show that \(f\left(x\right)=e^{2x}+2x^{2}-4\) has root in between \(0\) and \(1\).

  2. Show that a solution exists for \(2\sin\left(x\right)+1=0\) on the interval \([-\frac{\pi}{2}, \frac{3\pi}{2}]\).

  3. Show that \(x^{3}-10x=1\) has at least 3 solutions on the interval \([-4, 4]\).

  4. Show that \(f(x)=\pi x^{2}-4\) has two roots in \([-2, 2]\).

Continuity for Piecewise Functions

  1. What values of \(k\) makes \(f(x) = \begin{cases} kx^2 & \text{if } x \leq 2 \\ -kx + 18 & \text{if } x \gt 2 \end{cases}\) continuous everywhere.

  1. What values of \(a\) and \(b\) make \(f(x) = \begin{cases} x^3 & \text{if } x \leq 2 \\ ax + b & \text{if } 2 \lt x \lt \frac{5\pi}{2} \\ sin(x) & \text{if } x \ge \frac{5\pi}{2} \end{cases}\) continuous everywhere.

  1. What values of \(a\) and \(b\) make \(f(x) = \begin{cases} \frac{\left(2ax^{2}+ax+2\right)}{ax-4}& \text{if } x \leq 0 \\ 3ax+b & \text{if } 0 \lt x \lt 1 \\ \frac{\left(bx-6b\right)}{x} & \text{if } x \ge 1 \end{cases}\) continuous everywhere.

Q4/5: Computing Derivatives

Directly Computing Derivatives

  1. Find \(\frac{df}{dx}\) if \(f\left(x\right)=3x^{3}+\cos\left(x\right)-e^{\sin\left(x\right)}\).

  2. Find \(\frac{df}{dx}\) if \(f\left(x\right)=\tan\left(\arctan\left(x\right)+3x^{99}\right)e^{x}\)

  3. Find \(\frac{df}{dx}\) if \(f\left(x\right)=5^{\log_4\left(x\right)}+4^{x^{3}}\)

  4. Find \(\frac{df}{dx}\) if \(f\left(x\right)=\arcsin\left(x^{2}+x\right)\arctan\left(x^{3}\right)\arccos\left(x\right)\)

  1. Find \(\frac{df}{dx}\) if \(f\left(x\right)=\frac{\arctan\left(x\right)-3x^{2}}{2+5xe^{x}}\)

Check your answers using https://www.derivative-calculator.net/.

Derivatives of Inverse Functions

  1. Find \((f^{-1})'(x)\) if \(f\left(x\right)=x^{-3}-4\).

  2. Find \((f^{-1})'(x)\) if \(f\left(x\right)=6^x\).

  3. Find \((f^{-1})'(x)\) if \(f\left(x\right)=ln(x^5)\).

Logarithmic Differentiation

  1. Find \(\frac{df}{dx}\) if \(f\left(x\right)=ex^{ex}\).

  2. Find \(\frac{df}{dx}\) if \(f\left(x\right)=\sin\left(x\right)^{\cos\left(x\right)\tan\left(x\right)}\).

  3. Find \(\frac{df}{dx}\) if \(f\left(x\right)=f\left(x\right)=\frac{\left(x^{3}+2x+1\right)^{\pi}\left(e^{x}+\cos\left(x\right)\right)^{1142}}{\left(4x+5x^{5}\right)^{3}}\).

Check your answers using https://www.derivative-calculator.net/.

Implicit Differentiation

  1. Find \(\frac{dy}{dx}\) for \(3x^{2}y+2xy-y^{3}=0\)

  2. Find \(\frac{dy}{dx}\) for \(\arctan\left(x^{3}y\right)=3y\)

  3. Find \(\frac{dy}{dx}\) for \(\frac{xy+2}{x^{2}-y}=3y\)

  4. Find \(\frac{dy}{dx}\) for \(2^{x^2y^3}=x\)

Check your answers using https://calculator-derivative.com/implicit-differentiation-calculator.

Expanding Volumes

  1. The radius of a right circular cylinder is growing at a rate of \(2m/s\) while the volume remains the same. How fast is the height changing when the radius is \(1m\)?

  1. Suppose you have a right rectangular prism with a width that is increasing at \(3m/s\), height that is increasing at \(5m/s\), and a length that is decreasing at \(1m/s\). How much is the volume changing when the length, width, and height are all \(3m\)?

Sample solution is in practice test 1, question 6.

Ladder Problem

  1. A 5m ladder leaning against a wall has its base pushed towards the wall at a rate of \(4m/s\). At what rate was the ladder going up the wall when the base of the ladder was \(2m\) from the wall?

  1. An 8m ladder leaning against a wall is sliding down the wall at a rate of \(20m/s\). At what rate was the base sliding away from the wall when the ladder was \(5m\) from the bottom of the wall (height of 5m)?

  1. An extendible ladder leaning against a wall is being extended at a rate of \(2m/s\) and is sliding down the wall at a rate of \(1m/s\). How fast was the base of the ladder moving away from the wall when the ladder was \(6m\) from the bottom of the wall?

Sample solution is in practice test 3, question 6.

Cylindrical Tank

  1. A cylinder with radius 10m is being filled with a rate of \(3m/s\). How fast is the height changing?

  1. The height of water in a cylinder with radius 1m is falling at a rate of \(2m/s\). At what rate does the volume decrease?

Sample solution is in practice test 4, question 6.

Sailing Ships

  1. Ship A is 5km north of ship B. Ship A sails west at a rate of \(2km/h\) and ship B sails south at a rate of \(3km/h\). How fast is the distance between the ships changing after three hours?

  1. Ship A is 3km south, 5km east of ship B. Ship A sails north at a rate of \(2km/h\) and ship B sails east at a rate of \(1km/h\). How fast is the distance between the ships changing after an hour?

Sample solution is in practice test 2, question 6.

Tangent Line given a Point
